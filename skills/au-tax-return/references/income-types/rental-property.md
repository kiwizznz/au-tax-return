# ATO Rental Property Rules

Rules for individual taxpayers with residential investment/rental properties. All rules current for FY2024-25 and FY2025-26 unless otherwise noted.

> **Citation date:** May 2026. Verify current rules at [ato.gov.au/rental](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties).

**Covers:** Residential investment/rental properties — single or multiple, sole or co-owned (joint tenants / tenants in common), holiday and short-stay rentals (with private-use apportionment), and properties sold during the year (CGT event). Load this module whenever the user has any rental or investment property.

**Output additions:** this module contributes a `rental/{property-short-name}/` folder to the return, containing `rental-summary.md`, `rental-schedule.csv`, `ato-rental-guidance.md`, and `email-references-rental.md`.

## Intake — Details to Gather

When a user has a rental/investment property, gather the following for **each property** before scanning (this drives email search, apportionment, and CGT treatment):

- **Address** — to match emails and label the output folder
- **Settlement/purchase date** — determines which expenses fall in the FY and affects Div 40 second-hand rules (pre/post 9 May 2017)
- **Date first rented** — for apportionment if only rented part of the year
- **Property manager** — name/company, to target email searches
- **Ownership structure** — sole owner, joint tenants, or tenants in common (and what percentage)? Income and expenses must be split by legal ownership percentage
- **Was it ever your main residence?** — affects CGT treatment (6-year absence rule)
- **Was the property sold during this FY?** — if yes, settlement date and sale price; a CGT event requires cost base documentation, and sale-related costs (agent commission, conveyancing, marketing) are second-element cost base items
- **Any major work done this FY?** — renovations, solar, fit-out (distinguishes capital works from repairs)

## Rental Income

All of the following must be declared as assessable rental income:

| Income Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Gross rent received | Regular tenant payments, Airbnb/short-term rental income |
| Rent received in advance | Assessable in the year received |
| Bond retained | In lieu of unpaid rent or for damage repairs |
| Insurance payouts | Compensation for lost rent or reimbursement of claimed expenses |
| Letting/booking fees received | Fees paid by tenants |

**NOT assessable rental income:** Capital gains on sale (reported separately as CGT event).

> Source: [ATO — Rental income you must declare](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-income-you-must-declare) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Immediately Deductible Expenses

Deductible in full in the income year incurred, provided the property is rented or genuinely available for rent:

| Expense | Notes |
|---|---|
| Property manager fees & commissions | Management fees, letting fees |
| Advertising for tenants | Online listings, signage |
| Body corporate fees/levies | Admin fund and sinking fund contributions |
| Council rates | Municipal/local government rates |
| Water rates & charges | If landlord pays |
| Land tax | State/territory land tax |
| Landlord insurance | Building, contents, landlord protection |
| Loan interest | Only the portion used for the rental property |
| Repairs & maintenance | Restoring to original condition (see Repairs vs Improvements) |
| Pest control | Inspections and treatment |
| Gardening & lawn mowing | Regular maintenance only |
| Cleaning | Between tenants, regular cleaning |
| Legal expenses | Eviction costs, lease preparation (NOT purchase costs) |
| Tax agent/accountant fees | For the rental schedule portion of the return |
| Bank charges | On rental property account |
| Electricity/gas | If paid by landlord |
| Quantity surveyor fees | For depreciation schedule — 100% deductible |
| Assets costing $300 or less | Immediate write-off if used mainly for rental income |

**Travel to property — NOT DEDUCTIBLE (from 1 July 2017):**
Cannot claim travel to inspect, maintain, or collect rent for residential rental properties. This restriction remains in force.

> Source: [ATO — Common property expenses](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/common-property-expenses) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26
> Source: [ATO — Travel expenses](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses-you-can-t-claim/rental-properties-and-travel-expenses) | Changed 1 July 2017

## Repairs vs Improvements

This distinction is a top ATO audit target. Getting it wrong is the most common rental property error.

| Type | Tax Treatment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Repair** | Immediately deductible | Replacing damaged plasterboard with new plasterboard |
| **Improvement** | Capital — depreciate or add to cost base | Replacing fibro wall with brick feature wall |
| **Initial repair** | Capital — ALWAYS, even if unaware of defect | Fixing pre-existing problems discovered after purchase |

**The test:** Does it restore to original condition (repair) or make it better/different (improvement)?

- Replacing like-with-like = repair
- Upgrading materials or quality = improvement
- Kitchen/bathroom renovation = improvement
- Adding something new (deck, room, solar) = improvement

If repairs and improvements are done simultaneously, costs must be separated.

> Source: [ATO — Repair and maintenance expenses](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/repair-and-maintenance-expenses) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Division 40 — Plant & Equipment

Separately identifiable items with a limited effective life that are not part of the building structure.

### Common Residential Rental Effective Lives

| Asset | Effective Life | Diminishing Value | Prime Cost |
|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Air conditioning unit | 5 yr | 40% | 20% |
| Bathroom accessories | 3 yr | 66.67% | 33.33% |
| Blinds | 10 yr | 20% | 10% |
| Carpets & floor coverings | 8 yr | 25% | 12.5% |
| Curtains | 6 yr | 33.33% | 16.67% |
| Dishwasher | 8 yr | 25% | 12.5% |
| Exhaust fans | 10 yr | 20% | 10% |
| Furniture (freestanding) | 10–13 yr | varies | varies |
| Hot water system (electric/gas) | 12 yr | 16.67% | 8.33% |
| Hot water system (solar) | 15 yr | 13.33% | 6.67% |
| Lighting fixtures | 5 yr | 40% | 20% |
| Oven/cooktop | 12 yr | 16.67% | 8.33% |
| Rangehood | 10 yr | 20% | 10% |
| Security system/cameras | 4–5 yr | 40–50% | 20–25% |
| Smoke detectors | 6 yr | 33.33% | 16.67% |
| Washing machine/dryer | 10 yr | 20% | 10% |

### Depreciation Formulas

- **Prime cost:** `Cost x (days held / 365) x (100% / effective life)`
- **Diminishing value:** `Base value x (days held / 365) x (200% / effective life)`

Once chosen for an asset, you cannot switch methods.

### $300 Immediate Write-Off

Assets costing $300 or less: immediate deduction if used mainly for rental income, not part of a set over $300, and not one of multiple identical items totalling over $300.

### Low-Value Pool

Assets under $1,000 (at start of income year) can be pooled: 18.75% first year, 37.5% subsequent years.

### Second-Hand Plant Restrictions (from 9 May 2017)

**Critical date: 7:30 pm AEST, 9 May 2017**

| Scenario | Can Claim Div 40 on Second-Hand Items? |
|---|---|
| Property purchased BEFORE the critical date | Yes |
| Property purchased AFTER, second-hand items already in property | No |
| NEW items you purchase and install yourself | Yes — always |
| Brand new property, no prior owner claimed assets | Yes |
| Substantially renovated, no prior owner claimed assets | Yes |

The second-hand restriction does NOT affect Division 43 (capital works).

> Source: [ATO — Depreciating assets in rental properties](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/depreciating-assets-in-rental-properties) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26
> Source: [ATO — Second-hand depreciating assets](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/depreciating-assets-in-rental-properties/second-hand-depreciating-assets) | Changed 9 May 2017

## Division 43 — Capital Works

Structural/building costs, not separately identifiable items.

| Construction Commenced | Rate | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Before 22 August 1984 | Nil | No deduction |
| 22 Aug 1984 – 15 Sep 1987 | 4% | 25 years |
| After 15 September 1987 | 2.5% | 40 years |
| Build to Rent (50+ dwellings, construction after 9 May 2023, from 1 Jan 2025) | 4% | 25 years |

**What qualifies:** Building construction, structural improvements, renovations to building structure (walls, roof, floors, plumbing, wiring), driveways, retaining walls, fences.

**Key rules:**
- Deduction runs for 40 years from construction completion date, regardless of when you purchased
- Previous owner's claimed years reduce your remaining entitlement (the 40-year clock doesn't restart)
- NOT affected by the second-hand restrictions that apply to Division 40
- A quantity surveyor report is essential for second-hand properties where original builder records are unavailable

> Source: [ATO — Capital works deductions](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/capital-expenses/work-out-your-capital-works-deductions) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Borrowing Costs

Costs of establishing the loan for the rental property.

**What qualifies:** Loan establishment fees, lender's mortgage insurance (LMI), stamp duty on mortgage (not property), title search fees (lender), mortgage document preparation, mortgage broker fees, valuation fees for loan approval.

| Total Borrowing Costs | Treatment |
|---|---|
| $100 or less | Fully deductible in year incurred |
| More than $100 | Deducted over 5 years or the remaining period of the loan (whichever is shorter) |

Pro-rata for part-year: first year claim = amount x (days held / 365).

If the loan is repaid early, remaining unamortised balance is deductible in the year of repayment.

**NOT borrowing costs:** Annual loan package fees and account-keeping fees are immediately deductible each year as sundry rental expenses.

> Source: [ATO — Borrowing expenses](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/rental-expenses/borrowing-expenses) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Cost Base Items (CGT)

These are NOT deductible against rental income but form part of the cost base when the property is sold:

**First element — Acquisition costs:**
- Purchase price
- Stamp duty on property transfer
- Conveyancing/solicitor's fees on purchase
- Building/pest inspection fees before purchase

**Second element — Incidental costs:**
- Agent's commission on sale, legal fees on sale, advertising on sale

**Third element — Non-deductible holding costs:**
- Interest, rates, insurance etc. ONLY for periods the property was NOT rented and NOT your main residence

**Fourth element — Capital improvements:**
- Renovations, extensions reflected in property state at sale (to extent not already claimed as deduction)

**Key principle:** Amounts claimed (or that could have been claimed) as a deduction are EXCLUDED from cost base. No double-dipping.

> Source: [ATO — Cost base of assets](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/capital-gains-tax/calculating-your-cgt/cost-base-of-asset) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Capital Gains Tax

### 50% CGT Discount
- Must hold for at least 12 months
- After applying capital losses, remaining gain is halved
- Discounted amount added to assessable income at marginal rates

### 6-Year Absence Rule
If the property was your main residence before renting, you can treat it as your main residence for CGT purposes for up to 6 years after moving out, even if rented. Capital gain on sale during this period is fully exempt.

- Moving back resets the 6-year clock
- Can only have ONE main residence at a time for CGT purposes
- Choice is made when lodging the return for the year of sale (no advance nomination needed)

### First Income-Producing Use Rule
If the property was your main residence before first being rented (after 20 August 1996), market valuation at date of first rental becomes the cost base first element.

### Foreign Resident CGT Withholding (from 1 January 2025)
Rate increased to 15%, property value threshold removed. Sellers need an ATO clearance certificate to avoid 15% withholding by purchaser.

> Source: [ATO — CGT when selling your rental property](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/capital-gains-tax/property-and-capital-gains-tax/cgt-when-selling-your-rental-property) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26
> Source: [ATO — Treating former home as main residence](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/capital-gains-tax/property-and-capital-gains-tax/your-main-residence---home/treating-former-home-as-main-residence) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Apportionment

### Part-Year Rental
Expenses apportioned on a time basis: `Expense x (days rented or available / 365)`

### Vacancy Periods
- If genuinely available for rent (advertised, realistic rent, accepting tenants): deductions still claimable
- If NOT genuinely available (excessive rent, limited advertising, blocked periods): deductions must be reduced

### Private Use
If you or family/friends use the property privately, NO deduction for that period. Exclude private-use days from calculations. Holiday homes are a particular ATO audit focus.

### Interest on Refinanced/Redrawn Loans
If any part of the loan was refinanced or redrawn for private purposes, interest must be apportioned between investment and private portions. This continues for the life of the loan.

### Vacant Land (from 1 July 2019)
Cannot claim deductions for holding costs on vacant land (interest, rates, land tax, maintenance). Limited exceptions for land held by companies or used in business.

> Source: [ATO — Apportioning rental interest expenses](https://www.ato.gov.au/tax-and-super-professionals/for-tax-professionals/tax-professionals-newsroom/apportioning-rental-interest-expenses) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26
> Source: [ATO — Deductions for vacant land](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/land-vacant-land-and-subdividing/deductions-for-vacant-land) | Changed 1 July 2019

## Common Mistakes / ATO Audit Targets

The ATO's Random Enquiry Program found **96% of rental property returns had at least one error.** Estimated rental tax gap: **$1.3 billion.**

| Mistake | Details |
|---|---|
| Claiming repairs that are improvements | Kitchen/bathroom renos, material upgrades, initial repairs on purchase |
| Overclaiming loan interest | Must apportion if ANY part of loan redrawn for private use. ATO matches bank data |
| Claiming travel (post-2017) | Residential rental travel has been non-deductible since 1 July 2017 |
| Not apportioning for vacancy/private use | Holiday homes and properties not genuinely available for rent |
| Second-hand depreciation (post-May-2017) | Cannot claim Div 40 on existing plant in properties acquired after 9 May 2017 |
| Not declaring all income | Bond retained, insurance payouts, Airbnb income. ATO receives platform data |
| Incorrect co-owner allocation | Must split by legal ownership %, not by agreement |
| Claiming purchase costs as deductions | Stamp duty, conveyancing, inspections are cost base items |

**ATO data matching sources:** Financial institutions, property managers, rental bond authorities, sharing economy platforms, land titles offices, insurance companies, body corporate records, state revenue offices.

> Source: [ATO — Don't let your tax return be a fixer upper](https://www.ato.gov.au/media-centre/ato-warning-to-rental-property-owners-dont-let-your-tax-return-be-a-fixer-upper) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

## Record-Keeping

| Records | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Rental income and expense records | 5 years from date of lodgement |
| Acquisition, ownership, disposal records (for CGT) | 5 years after you sell the property |
| Records relating to an ATO dispute | Until dispute is resolved |

**What to keep:** Purchase contract, settlement statement, loan documents, all expense receipts, property manager statements, insurance policies, rate notices, body corporate notices, depreciation schedule, tenant leases, advertising records, evidence property was genuinely available for rent.

Paper or digital records acceptable. ATO myDeductions app can track rental income and expenses.

> Source: [ATO — Records for rental properties](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/investments-and-assets/property-and-land/residential-rental-properties/records-for-rental-properties-and-holiday-homes) | FY2024-25, FY2025-26

### Gmail Syntax Reference

Same syntax as `../email-search-common.md` — see that file for full Gmail search operator reference.

Key operators for rental property scanning:
- `from:domain.com.au` — sender domain
- `subject:(word1 OR word2)` — subject line search
- `after:YYYY/MM/DD before:YYYY/MM/DD` — date range
- `has:attachment` — emails with attachments
- `filename:pdf` — specific attachment types
- `"exact phrase"` — exact match
- Combine with AND (space) and OR
- Use parentheses for grouping
