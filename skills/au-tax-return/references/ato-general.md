## Tax Brackets

### 2024-25 and 2025-26 (identical)

| Taxable Income | Tax Rate | Tax on This Income |
|---------------|----------|-------------------|
| $0–$18,200 | 0% | Nil |
| $18,201–$45,000 | 16% | 16c per $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001–$135,000 | 30% | $4,288 + 30c per $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001–$190,000 | 37% | $31,288 + 37c per $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001+ | 45% | $51,638 + 45c per $1 over $190,000 |

Plus 2% Medicare levy on most taxable income.

### Future changes
- From 1 July 2026: 16% rate drops to 15%
- From 1 July 2027: drops further to 14%

## Work-From-Home Deductions

### Fixed Rate Method (recommended for simplicity)
- **2024-25 and 2025-26:** 70 cents per hour
- Covers: electricity, gas, internet, phone, stationery, computer consumables
- Can ALSO separately claim: depreciation of furniture/equipment, repairs
- CANNOT double-dip on items already covered by the rate

**Record-keeping required:**
- Total hours worked from home for the entire year (actual records, not estimates)
- At least one bill/receipt for each expense type the rate covers
- No dedicated home office required

### Actual Cost Method
- Calculate actual work-related portion of all running expenses individually
- Requires: all receipts/bills, total hours WFH, plus a representative 4-week diary
- Can include occupancy expenses (rent, mortgage interest) if dedicated home office
- Must apportion between work and private use

### Which method to recommend
- **Fixed rate** for most workers: simpler, fewer records, usually competitive
- **Actual cost** if high energy bills, expensive internet, or a dedicated room

## Depreciation Rules

### Items $300 or less (individuals)
- Immediate full deduction in the year of purchase
- Must be used mainly (>50%) for producing assessable income
- Must apportion if also used privately
- Must NOT be part of a set totalling over $300

### Items over $300
- Must depreciate over effective life
- Choose diminishing value (higher early deductions) or prime cost (even spread)
- The $20,000 instant asset write-off is for SMALL BUSINESSES ONLY, not individual employees

### Depreciation formulas
- **Prime cost:** `Cost × (days held ÷ 365) × (100% ÷ effective life)`
- **Diminishing value:** `Base value × (days held ÷ 365) × (200% ÷ effective life)`

### Common asset effective lives

| Asset | Effective Life | Prime Cost | Diminishing Value |
|-------|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Laptop/notebook | 2 years | 50% | 100% |
| Desktop computer | 4 years | 25% | 50% |
| Computer monitor | 4 years | 25% | 50% |
| Printer | 5 years | 20% | 40% |
| Mobile phone | 3 years | 33.33% | 66.67% |
| Headset | 4 years | 25% | 50% |
| Office desk | 10 years | 10% | 20% |
| Office chair | 10 years | 10% | 20% |
| Camera/video | 4 years | 25% | 50% |
| Power tools | 5 years | 20% | 40% |

Pro-rate for part-year: multiply by (days held ÷ 365), then by work-use percentage.

## Car & Travel

### Cents per Kilometre Method
- **2024-25 and 2025-26:** 88 cents per km
- Maximum: 5,000 km per car per year = **$4,400 max**
- No receipts required but must show how km were calculated
- Covers ALL car costs (fuel, rego, insurance, depreciation, maintenance)

### Logbook Method
- 12-week logbook required (valid for 5 years)
- Claim work-related % of ALL actual expenses
- No km limit — can yield significantly higher deductions
- Must keep ALL receipts

### What counts as work-related travel
- Between two separate workplaces
- To client sites (not your regular office)
- To conferences/training
- Carrying bulky equipment that can't be left at work

### NOT deductible
- Home to regular office (commuting — always private)
- Even if working overtime or unsociable hours

## Clothing & Uniforms

**Most office workers CANNOT claim conventional clothing.** Business attire, jeans, t-shirts — not deductible even if employer requires it or you only wear it to work.

**Can claim:**
- Compulsory uniforms with employer logo
- Protective clothing (hi-vis, steel-caps, safety glasses, etc.)
- Occupation-specific clothing (e.g., chef's checks, nurse's scrubs)
- Registered non-compulsory uniforms

**Laundry (if eligible clothing):**
- $1/load (work only) or $0.50/load (mixed)
- Up to $150 without written evidence

## Self-Education Expenses

The $250 reduction was REMOVED from 1 July 2022 — claim 100% of eligible expenses.

### Qualifying criteria
The education must either:
- Maintain or improve skills/knowledge for your CURRENT job, OR
- Be likely to lead to increased income from CURRENT employment

### Does NOT qualify if
- Only relates generally to current employment
- Enables you to get NEW/different employment
- Is not sufficiently connected to current work duties

### Claimable expenses
- Course/tuition fees
- Certification exam fees
- Conference registration fees
- Textbooks and study materials
- Travel to attend courses/exams/conferences
- Accommodation for overnight conferences (work portion only)

## Phone & Internet

**If using WFH fixed rate:** Already included — cannot claim separately.

**If NOT using fixed rate or not WFH:**
- Calculate work-related percentage of bills
- Need actual records showing work use throughout the year
- Bundled plans: separate the cost of each service first

**Mobile phone device:**
- $300 or less + mainly for work: immediate full deduction (work portion)
- Over $300: depreciate over 3-year effective life (work portion)

## Income Protection Insurance

- Premiums are **fully deductible**
- Must protect against loss of employment income
- If bundled with life/TPD, only the IP component is deductible
- Must be paid by you directly (not through super)
- Payouts are assessable income

Life insurance and TPD premiums are NOT deductible.

## Donations

- Must be to a Deductible Gift Recipient (DGR)
- Minimum $2 for monetary donations
- Must be a genuine gift (no material benefit in return)
- No upper cap on deductions
- Bucket collections: up to $10 without receipts

**NOT deductible:** GoFundMe (usually), raffle tickets, event tickets, charity auction purchases.

## Superannuation

### Concessional contributions cap
- **2024-25 and 2025-26:** $30,000/year
- Includes: employer SG + salary sacrifice + personal deductible contributions
- Taxed at 15% within super (vs marginal rate outside)

### Carry-forward unused cap
- Available if total super balance < $500,000 at 30 June of prior FY
- Unused cap amounts carry forward for up to 5 years

### Personal deductible contributions
- Make contribution to super fund, then lodge Notice of Intent
- Must receive acknowledgment BEFORE lodging tax return
- Tax saving = contribution × (marginal rate − 15%)

### Division 293 tax
- If income + concessional contributions > $250,000: additional 15% tax on excess

## Medicare Levy & Surcharge

### Medicare Levy: 2% of taxable income

### Medicare Levy Surcharge (MLS) — no private hospital cover

**2024-25:**

| Tier | Singles | MLS Rate |
|------|---------|----------|
| No MLS | ≤$97,000 | 0% |
| Tier 1 | $97,001–$113,000 | 1.0% |
| Tier 2 | $113,001–$151,000 | 1.25% |
| Tier 3 | $151,001+ | 1.5% |

**2025-26:**

| Tier | Singles | MLS Rate |
|------|---------|----------|
| No MLS | ≤$101,000 | 0% |
| Tier 1 | $101,001–$118,000 | 1.0% |
| Tier 2 | $118,001–$158,000 | 1.25% |
| Tier 3 | $158,001+ | 1.5% |

Family thresholds are double, +$1,500 per dependent child after the first.

## Substantiation & Record-Keeping

### $300 threshold
- **Total work expenses ≤ $300:** No receipts needed, but must have calculation records
- **Total work expenses > $300:** Must have written evidence for ALL claims

Excludes: car expenses, meal allowances, travel allowances (separate rules).

### Laundry sub-threshold
- Up to $150 without written evidence (still counts toward $300 total)

### Retention period
- Keep records for **5 years from the date you LODGE** your return

### Acceptable formats
- Paper or digital (photos, scans, digital receipts)
- ATO myDeductions app
- Spreadsheets and diary records
